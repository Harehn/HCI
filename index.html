Design Evolution:

Going from the alpha to the beta prototype, we changed the scope and implementation of the project. We went from having a static website into implementing our features as a chrome extension. This changed a lot of the scope and feasibility since we has limited time to implement this.

Design Change 1

Changing into extension

As stated earlier, we decided to change our product into a chrome extension instead of being on a dummy website. This change reflects our change in scope where we wanted to have better accessibility on a range of websites instead of only one dummy website.

This change matches the heuristics principle #2, match between System and the real world. We came to the realization that the dummy website is good to provide a demonstration of our ideas, it is not the ideal implementation in respect to real life.

Design Change 2

Focus on one type of color blindess, Deuteranomaly

Due to our late change in scope, we didn't have the leeway to implement all of the details that we wanted. We decided to try to focus on deuteranomaly.

From the existing resources and information we have, it is not as difficult to have changes for some of the other types of colorblindness.

However, this feature is non trivial with respect to our implementation.

In terms of feasibility,  we would need more time to target more types of colorblindess. Much of this limitation in the feasibility came from a late change in scope.

Design Change 3

Removal of sliders

Initially, the sliders were one of the ways we addressed the need for users to have the features better fit their needs. We had identified in our earlier observations that colorblindess is on a spectrum and a more flexible solution would work best.

However, as stated before we would need time to implement this.

In terms of feasibility,  we would need more time to implement this change. Much of this limitation in the feasibility came from a late change in scope.

Project reflection

Problem definition

As you can see in the diagram below, we had a lot of changes in the scope and in our project proposal.

Initially, we had a broad range of personas but then we narrowed down our focus on colorblindness. We also found that our problems were not unique to education websites. We later decided that an extension worked better than just working on a dummy site.

Usability goals

Our usability goals stayed more or less the same although we found goal 2 to be slightly harder to implement. Here are our usability goals.

1. Users should be able to easily navigate to the  accessibility setting in the website  chrome extension .

Rationale:  We want users to be able to easily access the desired features so they can make changes. We want to remove any confusion on how to reach the accessibility features.

2. Users should be able to preview the visual changes to the website.

Rationale:  Users might not be sure of how a change will affect the visuals of the website. They might need to try different types of settings before they are sure of the settings.

3. Users should have an easier time distinguishing the colors on the website after applying their desired settings.

Rationale: The end goal of our product is to make it easier for color blind to interact with the system. One of the ways to do that is to make it easier to distinguish colors.

While our usability goals stayed more or less the same, we had to tweak some of our goals to fit our scope and feasibility given the big changes within a tight time frame

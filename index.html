Problem Statement

Interfaces for learning online are clunky and often not easily accessible to people with disabilities such as visual impairment and learning impediments such as ADHD and dyslexia. This can be discouraging and impede the education of these groups.

One study by Milton Campoverde-Molina et al [1]  has shown that university websites around the world have a lot of problems regarding accessibility and some are difficult even for students without disabilities.

For example for color blindness, a study [2] finds designers failing in delivering any amount of support for color blind people.

Similarly for ADHD, educational websites can be evaluated for how well they can help with their design. [3]

Solution

Identify key aspects of the areas that cause issues to the target user groups. Make modular pieces of UI and features that can be mixed and matched to be implemented into other projects as well.

Users

Problems users had:

  1. Some of the color coded elements were ambiguous to the person with Deuteranomaly as they saw some of the green elements as red.
  2. The visually impaired user needed to take some breaks as the screen strained their eyes.

Implementation

Some key features that we think to implement to solve these issues are:

  1. Different color themes to help with different types of color blindness.
  2. Dark mode and brightness options to reduce strain on the eyes. (Websites have some effect on strain through the wavelength and visuals. [4])
  3. Option to adjust font size and some element ratios for better visibility.
  4. Ability to adjust visual clutter on the screen to personal preference.

Persona 1

Requin

Requin is a final year student who is doing his degree online. He is in Civil Engineering. He enjoys several aspects of his degree. He likes being social and so he often sets up calls with the other students in his class. Requin often uses his phone to access his university portal as he is on the move a lot. Requin has  Deuteranomaly. Some shades of green appear red to him.

Requin is 22 years old. He has a girlfriend who lives not far from his place. He had a part time job in the past but now only focuses on his studies.

Goals

Requin wants to use his university portal to focus on his assignments, course notes and lecture videos. He also wants to use the chat feature to stay in touch with his classmates. He also likes to keep track of his class progress.

Needs

Requin needs to finish his degree using the university portal. He needs to submit his assignment on time and read the relevant notes.

Barriers and Frustrations

When Requin checks in on his report and sees what he needs to do next for his classes, he needs to take more time because he is unable to see the color coding on the done assignments. This causes him some stress and panic.

Opportunities

Requin is able to alleviate his issues by manually keeping track of the work he does and talking to his professors.

Observations

Requin is able to use his university portal but some aspects cause him frustrations.

Persona 1

Eira

Eira is a 50 year old woman. She has a husband and 2 kids. She used to have an administrative job but she recently quit to continue her studies. She goes to class in person. She chose to study in a business course. She feels a bit out of place in the class but she is mature and is able to handle the environment and the workload well. She has to use the university portal for assignment submissions. She is somewhat tech literate so most of the basic features are not a problem to her. Her eyesight is also deteriorating.

Goals

Eira wants to use her university portal to focus on her assignments, course notes and lecture videos. She also likes to keep track of her class progress so she doesn’t fall behind.

Needs

Eira needs to finish her degree using the university portal. She needs to submit her assignment on time and read the relevant notes.

Barriers and Frustrations

Eira is able to use the website properly most of the time. However, she finds the font too small and has to get closer to the screen. She also has to take frequent breaks because of the strain on her eyes. She also finds that some features are nested too deep in some lists and it makes it difficult for her to find them.

Opportunities

Eira is able to work around not using the screen by printing out a lot of the long things she has to read like lecture notes. She often asks for help in the discussion forums and to her classmates about where to find the features she needs.

Use case 1

Eira goes onto her university portal. On her homescreen, she finds that the text too small to read. She finds an icon [ - aA + ] on the top of the screen . She can easily change the font size on her screen with the +/- buttons.

Alternatively, she can go in the settings, under Accessibility settings, she finds the option to change the settings and a sample text to see how this will affect her.

Now that the font size is just right for Eira, she doesn’t have to come too close to the screen.

Use case 2

Eira finds the bright screen too straining on her eyes. On the top of the screen she sees a moon icon with a toggle next to it. She clicks on it to turn on dark mode.  

Alternatively, she goes onto the settings which is available from any page on the portal and goes into the Accessibility settings and clicks on the dark mode option to turn it on.

Having dark mode makes Eira’s eyes feel less strained. She finds it easier to adapt to the screen now.

Design at high level

Pre-existing software and hardware:

  1. Text-to-speech
  2. Dyslexic font
  3. Night mode
  4. Video subtitles
  5. Font size adjustment
  6. Alt texts
  7. Choose better color coding
  8. Limiting clutter

Everyone has different needs to make an education website more accessible. Usually, it would be expected to go through an accessibility setting menu and allow tweaking. It would be helpful to the user as well if they could see a preview of the specific changes.

Two examples can be seen here. [5][6]

       

However in some cases, some users might have trouble locating the menu. For example, if the font is too small, it would be difficult to see the options. In these cases, some accessibility options should be readily available. Two examples are given here. The first one is a common text font size adjustment. The second is a night mode toggle.

       

Among the many different components in education websites, we identified 4 which are common and essential:

  1. Videos (Lecture recording)
  2. Announcement (Some form of text for instructions and messages)
  3. PDF viewing (Uploaded lecture notes or readings)
  4. Assignment submission

Other non-essential components would be chat functions, discussion threads, file navigation and grade viewing. Amongst the above, file navigation would be one of the candidates to also improve.

One of our design directions are how many components of the above would cause too much clutter and how the arrangement would help with attention, visual clutter and visibility.

Another design direction would be limiting and streamlining the number of possible navigations/pages. For example, having too many accessible pages might mean that some important pages such as a group tab or assignment tab will be missed or ignored.

Justifying Feasibility

Available resources

Many accessibility options have been created and applied to many other websites. However, many education websites are lacking these options. In addition, the design, color choices and visual aspects of these websites can be inconvenient to users without disability as well.

We plan to use websites with accessibility options as reference and see how it applies to education websites.

Specific goals

  1. We want to consolidate the various simple accessibility options like font size adjustment and see how to apply them to preexisting websites.
  2. Adjust color themes and combinations to decide which work best for different types of visually impaired users (or other users)
  3. Make modular sections and designs that can easily be ported to pre existing content.

Skills

The two major requirements for this product are web development and design.

For web development, there will be heavy focus on the front end and not much on the back end. We also have to tackle the goal of making easily portable concepts.

The second requirement is going to be design. There needs to be empathy towards people with  learning disabilities and visual impairment. We need to iterate and find what works best for people with different needs.

We intend to use paper prototypes extensively to test out different design, configurations and possibly specifics like color themes.

Activities

The activities we would spend time on would be research (on existing products and papers on accessibility), paper prototyping, user testing and high fidelity prototyping (through website mockups). We intend to make several iterations with paper prototyping and user testing.

Feasibility

The most time consuming part to complete our goals would be to create the front ends of these designs. We believe it is realistic to have high fidelity prototypes of several of these designs by the end of the semester

Test Plan Critique

The evaluating team presents a thorough, complete and clear critique of the methodology used for the usability evaluation and the criteria to be assessed, e.g., referring to specific heuristics from Nielsen's list. Modifications to the usability evaluation plan (if any) are likely are likely to produce useful data and apply criteria that are appropriate for evaluating the system. If present, obvious deficiencies but also minor deficiencies in the originally proposed plan are addressed with modifications.

There are several problems presented in the test plan.

User testing:
For the data sheet, since most of the tasks could be done within 1 minute, the time taken should not be rounded to the nearest minute when doing the test. It allows better analysis on how the users perform in each task.

Evaluation plan:
For the usability evaluation, the evaluation criteria are described and justified. However, some related heuristics are not included. On the other hand, a discussion session could be included after the evaluator assessing the system individually, so that they could synthesize the ideas and discuss what are the major problems.

Design Critique

The evaluating team suggested several possible changes based on the user testing, all of which were important and directly addressed the problems identified in user testing.

Home page

Some users suggested that they have trouble understanding how to use the system when they first get on the website, especially for the usage of the map. It is suggested that a simple tutorial could be included. For example, there could be some indications and descriptions on how to navigate and use the website.

Navigations

As found in the user testing and evaluation, it is good to have the “back” buttons so that users can easily go backward when they click any wrong buttons. However, it would be even better if the hyperlinks could be incorporated on the top navigation bar. For example, there is “H2X 1A1 / News / Quiz”on the top bar, it would be better if it is able to directly go back to the “H2X 1A1” so that users can quickly select other options for that postal code. Moreover, it would be better to include a link or button for the home page.

Presentation of information

There are users suggested that it is difficult to search for a particular        election event or representative on the website. It is suggested that functions like searching, sorting or filtering could be applied so that users could find the desired information more efficiently. Moreover, instead of showing the information in a column with the application of horizontal scrolling, having vertical scrolling could be better for both mobile phone users and computer users. It is because more information could be shown at once, and horizontal scrolling is less user-friendly for computer users. For the achievements part that is shown on the user profile, the difficulties of looking for a particular achievement is also identified. It is suggested that the achievements could be classified in a better way, so that they could be shown under different classification, and could be hidden and shown to avoid showing a long list at once.

Gamification

Since some of the users found the quizzes for news stories boring, it is suggested other ways besides quizzes could be used for testing their understanding, or more variations could be added to the quizzes.

Overall

We want to emphasize the significance of understanding the diverse user base, and we genuinely appreciate the thought-provoking aspects of your presentation. Your categorizations of various user types and participations are genuinely intriguing, showcasing your dedication to defining these user personas thoughtfully. However, to ensure the ultimate success of your project, it's essential to bridge the gap between these well-crafted persona definitions, empathy maps, the low-fidelity prototype, and the final computer prototype.

From our perspective, the challenge lies in clearly addressing the values and needs of the various user targets. The current procedures do not cater to the unique requirements of each user group. It's not just about building a prototype; it's about crafting an experience that resonates with your intended audience.

Research has underlined the critical role of capturing users' attention within the first six seconds. To that end, we recommend developing a prototype that can instantly engage different user targets. This alignment between your concept and its execution can significantly amplify the overall impact of your project.

Moreover, tracking user engagement and understanding the elements that make the content exciting and engaging is paramount. The ability to track interactions, such as user clicks on links to access other articles, can provide invaluable insights for enhancing the effectiveness of your project.

Creating an environment where users can interact freely with the platform without predefined restrictions or assumptions is equally essential. This approach can reveal valuable information about how users navigate different sections, the frequency of their clicks on links for additional information, and whether they encounter any bottlenecks or issues in their journey.

One area that requires attention is the apparent lack of specific features that guide users through the process, especially when they face hurdles or are uncertain about the next steps. Incorporating elements to facilitate smoother navigation and user guidance is critical to enhancing the overall user experience.

In addition, conducting user observations is vital, especially with potential users who represent various age groups and backgrounds. This approach can provide a holistic perspective on how diverse users interact with your platform, enabling you to fine-tune it to meet their unique needs.

We commend your focus on politically engaged individuals and those with knowledge backgrounds. Presenting a platform for engaging in political games and elections holds immense promise.

However, we noticed that one area that could benefit from enhancement is adding a designated space for users to provide comments and express their preferences. Enabling this level of interaction can significantly boost user engagement and satisfaction. It will also create a valuable space for knowledge-sharing and meaningful employment, enriching the user experience.

Moreover, it's crucial to address the concept of minimalist design. While you mention it, we couldn't find any related tasks, objectives, or goals demonstrating its integration into your design. Minimalist design emphasizes simplicity, clarity, and the removal of unnecessary elements, and it's vital to have precise tasks, objectives, and goals aligned with this design approach. Providing insight into how minimalism enhances the user experience and specifying related tasks can lead to a more coherent and user-friendly design.

Furthermore, we'd like to gain a better understanding of how you've incorporated gamification into your project. While you've mentioned it, we seek clarification about how it's applied to your tasks. We are particularly interested in whether there are quizzes and scores, but there isn't a clear link between these elements and your observation goals. Clear objectives and procedures related to gamification can help users comprehend its role in their engagement.

Finally, considering the diverse user targets in your project, it's vital to create experiments tailored to each group. Instead of a one-size-fits-all approach, consider designing specific experiments that evaluate your design for different, highly distinct user categories. This approach can provide a more nuanced understanding of how your project caters to user needs and preferences.

Additionally, it's crucial to consider the presence of algorithms within your project that facilitate the recall of user preferences and their interaction history. Users often appreciate the ability to revisit links they've previously seen and to understand what has occurred before. However, we need help finding specific elements within your project that address this critical feature.

It's worth exploring the inclusion of mechanisms that enable users to recall their preferences and interaction history. These features can significantly enhance the user experience and provide valuable functionality for your platform. Clarity in this regard can contribute to a more robust and user-friendly design.

We believe that your project holds great potential, and with these enhancements, it can become an even more captivating and compelling platform.

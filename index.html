Design Evolution:

Going from the alpha to the beta prototype, we changed the scope and implementation of the project. We went from having a static website into implementing our features as a chrome extension. This changed a lot of the scope and feasibility since we had limited time to implement this.

Design Change 1

Changing into extension

As stated earlier, we decided to change our product into a chrome extension instead of being on a dummy website. This change reflects our change in scope where we wanted to have better accessibility on a range of websites instead of only one dummy website.

This change matches the heuristics principle #2, match between System and the real world. We came to the realization that the dummy website is good to provide a demonstration of our ideas, it is not the ideal implementation in respect to real life.

Design Change 2

Focus on one type of colorblindness, Deuteranomaly

Due to our late change in scope, we didn't have the leeway to implement all of the details that we wanted. We decided to try to focus on deuteranomaly.

From the existing resources and information we have, it is not as difficult to have changes for some of the other types of colorblindness.

However, this feature is non trivial with respect to our implementation.

In terms of feasibility,  we would need more time to target more types of colorblindness. Much of this limitation in the feasibility came from a late change in scope.

Design Change 3

Removal of sliders

Initially, the sliders were one of the ways we addressed the need for users to have the features better fit their needs. We had identified in our earlier observations that colorblindness is on a spectrum and a more flexible solution would work best.

However, as stated before we would need time to implement this.

In terms of feasibility,  we would need more time to implement this change. Much of this limitation in the feasibility came from a late change in scope.

Design Change 4

Preset colour adjustment
We have decided to have a preset value change of saturation and contrast, which is believed to be a good alternative for sliders. While sliders provide the flexibility, sometimes it could be difficult for users to get a suitable setting over the spectrums of different colour attributes. Although having a one-clicked button to adjust the colour automatically may not provide a well-fitted solution for the users, it is much easier and simpler to be used.

Project reflection

Problem definition

As you can see in the diagram below, we had a lot of changes in the scope and in our project proposal.

Initially, since our understanding of the problem space is not good enough, we had a broad range of personas. But then we tried to make the solution more specific by better defining the target users and problems. We narrowed down our focus on colorblindness. We also found that our problems were not unique to education websites, and later decided that an extension could work better than just working on a dummy site.

Usability goals

As the project processes, our usability goals become more specific as we understand better for the users. Therefore, our usability goals were kept refining in order to provide a better solution that aligns users’ needs. Here is the evolution of our usability goals.



At the beginning of the project:


Easy to perceive

Goal:  “Users should be able to perceive correct information based on different visual or non-visual elements”

Easy to use

Goal: “Users should be able to navigate the website to perform all possible tasks without any difficulties after a reasonable time of exploration”

Understandable

Goal: “Users should be able to read and comprehend the web content with reasonable efforts”

Flexible to customize

Goal:  “Users should be able to customize different elements based on their personal preference that helps better use of the website to learn efficiently and effectively”

Later:

1. Users should be able to easily navigate to the  accessibility setting in the website  chrome extension .

Rationale:  We want users to be able to easily access the desired features so they can make changes. We want to remove any confusion on how to reach the accessibility features.

2. Users should be able to preview the visual changes to the website.

Rationale:  Users might not be sure of how a change will affect the visuals of the website. They might need to try different types of settings before they are sure of the settings.

3. Users should have an easier time distinguishing the colors on the website after applying their desired settings.

Rationale: The end goal of our product is to make it easier for color blind to interact with the system. One of the ways to do that is to make it easier to distinguish colors.

Since the prototype is revised and evolved by removing the sliders due to the feasibility, we no longer stay with the 2nd goal. While our usability goals stayed more or less the same, in order to fit our scope and feasibility given the big changes within a tight time frame, some of our goals should be further refined.

User Experiences

We believe that installing and using the google chrome extension should be easy and useful for our users. Not only could users use it on educational websites to better keep track of their studies, but also allow them to learn better with different learning materials distributed on the Internet. Users could better distinguish colours on different photos, graphs and data representations, which could make their learning easier and more enjoyable.
